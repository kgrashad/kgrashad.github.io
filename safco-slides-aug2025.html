Technology Can't Fix Culture
The Problem:
Employees found ways to work around limitations. Introducing technology without changing legacy incentives and rules limits value.
Real Example at Talabat:
Old Way
Ship every few months
New Process
Weekly "Agile" sprints
Result
Still delivered every few months
They changed the process and tools, not the rules and incentives
What We Actually Changed
1
End-to-End Squads
5 people owning problems completely
Tech + Commercial/Finance/Etc together
2
Simple Rule
Ship to customers every 1-2 weeks
Present learning. No exceptions.
3
Leadership Involved
CEO, COO, CTO, CFO directly engaged
Cut through middle management
4
Top Alignment
Tough discussions at leadership level
Don't leave teams to resolve conflicts

What We Learned
1. Customer Value First
Ask "what problems can we solve?" not "what technology to adopt?"
2. Iterate Fast
Learn in weeks what planning takes months
3. Culture Over Technology
Change rules and incentives, not just processes
4. Small End-to-End Teams
5 people, all functions, ship every 1-2 weeks
5. Leadership Hands-On
Align at the top first, cut through middle management
